FAQ

Questions worth a straight answer.

Does NearChat need internet?
No. The chat path runs entirely on your local Wi-Fi. The only thing that touches the internet is the optional update check (App Store / Play Store) and any external link a peer sends you that you choose to open.
Where are messages stored?
Only on your device, in an AES-GCM-encrypted SQLite database. The encryption key lives in iOS Keychain / Android Keystore. There is no cloud copy.
What if my friend isn't on the same Wi-Fi?
Then NearChat won't deliver. By design. We don't have a relay server. Most people use it with family at home, friends at the office, or roommates — anyone you share a router with.
What happens if my friend's phone gets a new IP?
NearChat re-discovers them automatically by scanning your local /24 subnet on the chat port. You don't have to re-pair when the router shuffles DHCP leases.
Do you collect anything?
No. There is no analytics SDK, no crash reporter, no telemetry. The privacy page explains every byte of network traffic the app generates.
Can I unlock the app with Face ID?
Yes, optionally. Set a password first, then turn on biometrics. The password hash never leaves the device, and biometric data never enters the app — iOS / Android handle it.
